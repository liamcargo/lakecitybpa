Spread Awareness today!

Sharing your mental health stories on social media platforms can be a powerful way to connect with others and foster understanding. There are numerous options at your disposal, such as YouTube, where you can create in-depth videos that detail your experiences and insights. Each platform/organization has its unique audience and style, so feel free to choose the one that resonates most with you.

Whether you opt for a personal story, informative content, or a discussion about mental health challenges, your voice can help others feel less alone and inspire them to share their own experiences. By sharing on social media, you contribute to a broader narrative that promotes awareness and encourages open dialogue about mental health topics. Remember, your story matters, and it could make a significant difference insomeone else's life.

Call Lines and Organizations

There are many sources around you to help you whether it is suicide awareness, or any Mental Health Disorders and Illnesses. 988 is the BEST way to contact someone with Suicidal thoughts. [If someone has committed suicide or injured themselves, please call 911 Immediatley.]

There are many organizations that help with Mental Awareness such as Sources of Strengths and National Alliance of Mental Illnesses. These organizations help promote and fundraise for Mental Health awareness locally and nationwide. See How you can help today!
